Recommendation and Investment View

Too early; proceed to confirmatory DD only

AISAN is not a classic leverage-driven LBO. The more credible framing is a growth-oriented take-private of a niche geospatial / surveying software and Mobility / DX platform, where value creation depends on cleaner governance, a higher-quality revenue mix, productization of point-cloud and local-government DX workflows, disciplined Mobility / DX project selection and careful use of excess cash.

The model does not support paying an unconstrained premium. At the default 30% premium, the Sponsor case falls short of a 2.0x MOIC target. A bid should proceed only to confirmatory DD, with a final offer conditioned on investigation resolution, audited FY2026 numbers, segment-level revenue quality, cash availability and shareholder path.

Industry Backdrop

Geospatial, Construction DX and Local Mobility

The strategic thesis is tied to public infrastructure renewal, construction productivity, 3D point-cloud adoption, high-precision maps and local-government mobility projects. The issue for PE underwriting is not whether these themes are attractive; it is whether AISAN captures them through durable, high-margin, repeatable revenue rather than lumpy projects or low-margin hardware pass-through.

Market Valuation and Trading Performance

Current Valuation Is Not an Obvious Bargain After a Take-Private Premium

At the latest sourced close of JPY 1,680, gross market capitalization is approximately JPY 9.3bn. A 30% premium implies an offer price of JPY 2,184 and headline entry EV / estimated FY2025 EBITDA of 13.1x. That is not too expensive if Mobility / DX scales and margins improve, but it is too demanding if the investigation creates restatement risk or if Mobility revenue proves project-based and volatile.

Take-Private Rationale

Why Private Ownership Could Matter

  • Reduce public-company friction and allow management to remediate governance without quarterly-market pressure.
  • Shift capital allocation from cash accumulation to product investment, selective M&A and shareholder value creation.
  • Professionalize segment reporting, backlog analytics, pricing and recurring-revenue KPIs.
  • Support Mobility / DX through partnerships where public-company volatility or small-cap liquidity may constrain strategic moves.

Value-Up Plan

Potential Value Creation Theses

  • Convert more of the public surveying software base into recurring maintenance, cloud modules and data services.
  • Separate Mobility / DX project revenue from durable platform revenue; keep low-margin one-off projects disciplined.
  • Use private ownership to remove listing cost, simplify governance and reinvest behind product management and sales coverage.
  • Rationalize cash: retain operating liquidity, but evaluate buyback-equivalent recapitalization or post-close dividend only after legal and lender review.
  • Pursue targeted partnerships or bolt-ons in point-cloud processing, local government DX and high-precision map data.

Final Recommendation

Proceed to Confirmatory DD Only

Governance and accounting-related matters are a gating item. No binding final bid should be submitted until the special investigation report, audit conclusions, any restatements, subsidiary controls remediation and FY2026 audited financials are reviewed.

Subject to that gating item, AISAN could be interesting as a small-cap growth buyout at a disciplined premium, particularly if DD confirms recurring software revenue, defensible segment margins, usable excess cash and a credible Mobility / DX backlog. If the seller requires a 40-50% premium, or if Mobility / DX revenue quality is weak, the economics do not appear compelling for a financial sponsor.

Model Methodology

  • Projection begins with FY2025A segment revenue and applies five-year scenario growth paths.
  • EBITDA is calculated from revenue margin assumptions plus public-company cost savings.
  • D&A is estimated at 3.0% of revenue; capex at 3.5% of revenue; working capital at 10.0% of revenue growth.
  • Entry debt is constrained to scenario debt / estimated FY2025 EBITDA, not to the underwritten exit case.
  • Exit equity value equals exit enterprise value less net debt; cash above debt increases exit equity value.
